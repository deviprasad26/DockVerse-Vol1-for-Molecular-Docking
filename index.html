Molecular Docking

🔬 How Molecular Docking Works — Receptor & Ligand Explained

Receptor (Protein Target): The biological macromolecule you want your drug to bind — an enzyme, kinase, or receptor. Enter its 4-letter PDB ID from rcsb.org (e.g. 1HVR = HIV-1 protease, 6LU7 = COVID-19 Mpro). Vina preprocesses the protein: removes water, adds polar hydrogens, assigns Gasteiger charges.

Ligand (Drug): The small FDA-approved drug molecule docked into the receptor's binding pocket. Select from the dropdown — SMILES auto-fills from PubChem. Vina exhaustively searches all rotatable bond conformations inside the search box to find the lowest binding energy.

Result: Binding affinity in kcal/mol — more negative = stronger binding. −8 to −10 is excellent drug-target binding.
